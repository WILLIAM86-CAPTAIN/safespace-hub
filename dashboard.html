Validated Screening

Clinically Validated Assessment Tools

All tools follow established clinical protocols. Results are for informational purposes only — not a diagnosis.

PHQ-9 Depression

Patient Health Questionnaire — 9 items assessing depression symptoms over the past 2 weeks. Widely used in clinical settings worldwide.

PHQ-99 Questions~3 min

GAD-7 Anxiety

Generalized Anxiety Disorder Scale — 7 questions measuring anxiety severity. Also effective for PTSD, panic disorder, and social anxiety.

GAD-77 Questions~2 min

PCL-5 PTSD

PTSD Checklist for DSM-5 — 20 items assessing PTSD symptoms across intrusion, avoidance, cognition/mood, and arousal clusters.

PCL-520 Questions~5 min

MDQ Bipolar Screening

Mood Disorder Questionnaire — validates possible bipolar disorder by assessing past manic/hypomanic episodes and their functional impact.

MDQ13 Questions~3 min
These screening tools are validated clinical instruments used for informational purposes only. They are not substitutes for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Always consult a qualified mental health professional for diagnosis and treatment planning.

🛡️ Build Your Personal Safety Plan

1

Warning Signs

Know your personal warning signs — thoughts, feelings, or behaviors that mean a crisis may be developing.

2

Internal Coping

Things you can do on your own to distract or soothe yourself — breathing, movement, creativity.

3

Social Distractions

People and places that provide distraction — not necessarily talking about the crisis, just connection.

4

Trusted People

People you can ask for help. Write their names and numbers. Keep this list accessible.

5

Professionals

Your therapist, doctor, or crisis line. Have their contacts saved. Don't wait until the worst moment.

6

Safe Environment

Remove or reduce access to means. Make your environment safer during difficult periods.

An Adaptive, Inclusive Mental Wellness Platform

The Safe Space Hub was designed from the ground up with one priority: making evidence-based mental health support accessible to everyone, especially those who need it most — individuals with Autism Spectrum Disorder, PTSD, high anxiety, and chronic stress.

Every design decision — from color contrast ratios to animation speeds — was made with neurodivergent and trauma-impacted users in mind. We follow WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards, mobile-first responsive design, and HIPAA-aware data handling principles.

All screening tools implement validated clinical instruments: PHQ-9, GAD-7, PCL-5, and MDQ. Your data never leaves your device — assessments run entirely client-side.
